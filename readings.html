Beyond the Papers

Readings & Reflections

A curated collection of books and essays that have shaped my thinking — on science, society, ethics, and what it means to be human.

I believe good science requires broad reading. The books below have shaped how I think about climate, ethics, power, and the human condition. Where I've written reflections, I've linked them — otherwise I share a brief thought on what stayed with me.

Fiction & Literary
🪲
Fiction
Franz Kafka
The Metamorphosis
"Even after waking up as a bug, Gregor's first thoughts are not about fear or freedom, but about work — missing the train, disappointing his employer."
What struck me most was not the transformation itself, but how little it changed anything fundamental. A meditation on usefulness, isolation, and quiet replacement — disturbingly familiar for anyone in a high-pressure academic environment.
★★★★★
Read Reflection →
🌿
Speculative Fiction
Kazuo Ishiguro
Never Let Me Go
"An exploration of identity, humanity, and the ethics of scientific advancement in a hauntingly familiar world."
Ishiguro asks what we owe to those whose lives we engineer, and whether knowledge of one's fate makes compliance more or less tragic. A book I keep returning to when thinking about bioethics and the governance of science.
★★★★★
Science & Non-fiction
🌍
Climate Science
Elizabeth Kolbert
The Sixth Extinction
The book that first convinced me that climate science must be communicated urgently and accessibly, not just published in journals. Kolbert's field reportage is a model for science writing I aspire to.
★★★★★
🌱
Philosophy of Science
Thomas S. Kuhn
The Structure of Scientific Revolutions
Essential reading for any scientist. Kuhn's concept of paradigm shifts changed how I think about the slow acceptance of new climate modeling approaches and why "credibility" in science is a social as much as an epistemic question.
★★★★★
🧭
Philosophy
Immanuel Kant
Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals
Dense but transformative. Kant's categorical imperative directly informs my current work on rethinking Kantian principles for climate action — the idea that ethical principles must be universalisable resonates deeply with global climate justice.
★★★★☆
