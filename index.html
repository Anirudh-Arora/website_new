About Me

Climate Scientist
in the making

🌍 IISER Bhopal → KAUST, Saudi Arabia
Final-year BS at IISER Bhopal · Now a visiting researcher at KAUST's CLIMATics Group, co-supervised by Prof. Ibrahim Hoteit for my master's thesis.

My work sits at the intersection of atmospheric dynamics, solar geoengineering, and climate model evaluation. I work with large CMIP5/CMIP6 multi-model ensembles, GeoMIP simulations, and reanalysis datasets to understand how the climate system responds — and how well our models capture it.

Beyond modelling, I think deeply about the socio-political dimensions of climate action: how scientific evidence translates into governance, and what philosophical frameworks can guide equitable climate policy.

What I Study

Research Interests

🌐

Climate Model Evaluation

Assessing GCM performance and credibility — especially CMIP5/CMIP6 ensembles — over South Asia and the Global South, where model uncertainty is greatest.

☁️

Solar Geoengineering

Investigating atmospheric and regional climate consequences of stratospheric aerosol injection (SAI) using GeoMIP G1 and G6sulfur simulations.

🌧️

Indian Summer Monsoon

Understanding the dynamics, variability, and future projections of the Indian monsoon — and how geoengineering interventions alter its character.

🏭

Aerosols & Air Quality

Multi-scale analysis of natural and anthropogenic aerosol sources over the Indo-Gangetic Plain and their interactions with clouds and radiative forcing.

📜

Climate Policy & Decision Theory

Principled frameworks for sequential climate decision-making using MDPs, and the ethics of geoengineering governance.

🧭

Science, Society & Philosophy

Exploring socio-political dimensions of climate action — from transboundary carbon migration to Kantian frameworks for public enlightenment.
