Research Focus

My work sits at the intersection of atmospheric dynamics, solar geoengineering, and climate model evaluation. I work with large CMIP5/CMIP6 multi-model ensembles, GeoMIP simulations, and reanalysis datasets to understand how the climate system responds — and how well our models capture it.

I am particularly drawn to questions about the Indian summer monsoon, stratospheric aerosol injection, and the credibility of global climate models over the Global South — a region critically underrepresented in the literature.

Beyond modelling, I think deeply about the socio-political dimensions of climate action: how scientific evidence translates into governance, and what philosophical frameworks can guide equitable climate policy.

Research Interests

🌐

Climate Model Evaluation

Assessing the performance and credibility of GCMs — especially CMIP5/CMIP6 ensembles — over South Asia and the Global South, where model uncertainty is greatest.

☁️

Solar Geoengineering

Investigating the atmospheric and regional climate consequences of stratospheric aerosol injection (SAI) using GeoMIP G1 and G6sulfur simulations.

🌧️

Indian Summer Monsoon

Understanding the dynamics, variability, and future projections of the Indian monsoon — and how geoengineering interventions alter its character.

🏭

Atmospheric Aerosols & Air Quality

Multi-scale analysis of natural and anthropogenic aerosol sources over the Indo-Gangetic Plain and their interactions with cloud microphysics and radiative forcing.

📜

Climate Policy & Decision Theory

Developing principled frameworks for sequential climate decision-making using Markov Decision Processes, and examining the ethics of geoengineering governance.

🧭

Science, Society & Philosophy

Exploring the socio-political dimensions of climate action — from transboundary carbon migration to Kantian frameworks for public enlightenment and climate ethics.
